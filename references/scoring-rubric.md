# Scoring Rubric

Score each direction from 1 to 5.

## 1. Distinctiveness
1 = feels generic or category-obvious
3 = has a visible twist but still familiar
5 = immediately distinct in worldview or framing

## 2. Brand fit
1 = clashes with brand posture
3 = acceptable with adjustment
5 = feels native to the brand while still distinctive

## 3. Execution ease
1 = difficult to execute cleanly
3 = feasible with work
5 = production-ready with minimal ambiguity

## 4. Scalability
1 = one-off only
3 = can extend modestly
5 = can expand into a system or series

## 5. Benchmark-fit quality
1 = ignores useful performance structure
3 = partially aligned
5 = structurally strong without imitation

## 6. Channel suitability
1 = awkward for target platform
3 = workable
5 = naturally legible in the platform rhythm

## 7. Originality risk
1 = low risk of mimicry
3 = moderate similarity pressure
5 = high derivative danger

## Recommendation
Use this formula when comparing directions:
- total score = distinctiveness + brand fit + execution ease + scalability + benchmark-fit quality + channel suitability + (6 - originality risk)

## Interpretation
- 30+ = strong winner candidate
- 24-29 = good but needs pressure test
- 18-23 = usable, not share-worthy
- under 18 = reject or regenerate
