# Further Reading

This repository is inspired by research and practice around design fixation, divergent/convergent ideation, and Human-AI co-creation.

## Human-AI co-creation and divergence/convergence

1. Exploration vs. Fixation: Scaffolding Divergent and Convergent Thinking for Human-AI Co-Creation with Generative Models
   - arXiv result discovered during repository preparation
   - highly aligned with the operational logic behind this repo

2. Collaborative Ideation Partner: Design Ideation in Human-AI Co-creativity
   - DOI: 10.5220/0010640800003060
   - relevant for AI as an ideation collaborator rather than a one-shot generator

3. Human-AI Co-Creation Systems in Design and Art
   - DOI: 10.63282/3050-9416.ijaibdcms-v5i1p111
   - useful for situating creative collaboration patterns with AI

4. The Mosaic of Human-AI Co-Creation: Emerging human-technology relationships in a co-design process with generative AI
   - DOI: 10.35542/osf.io/g5fb8
   - helpful for understanding relationship modes between human and model

## Design fixation and creativity

5. Where next for research on fixation, inspiration and creativity in design?
   - DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2017.02.001
   - a useful framing resource for design fixation as a recurring creativity problem

6. Using Evolved Analogies to Overcome Creative Design Fixation
   - DOI: 10.1007/978-0-85729-224-7_6
   - relevant to the use of remote analogies in divergence mode

7. Examining design fixation in engineering idea generation: the role of example modality
   - DOI: 10.1080/21650349.2013.774689
   - useful for understanding how examples can help or constrain ideation

8. Enhancing Creativity by Demonstrating Individual Vulnerability to Fixation
   - DOI: 10.35199/icdc.2020.08
   - useful as a mindset reminder: fixation is common, not exceptional

## Practical takeaway for this repo

These references support a few practical operating rules:
- do not ask for the final artifact too early
- separate generation from evaluation
- use examples and benchmarks structurally, not imitatively
- deliberately create multiple directions before refinement
- use distant analogies to break local optimum traps
